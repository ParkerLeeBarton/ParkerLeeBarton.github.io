The Purdue SigBots is a highly respected 70+ member interdisciplinary competitive robotics team. The Sigbots fall under the umbrella organization (ACM) Association for Computing Machinery.

Workspace Overhaul

Figure 2: Fusion 360 Render of Initial Concept

The Purdue Sigbots has had the lab space in the basement of HAAS hall for over 15 years. Through that time the lab has had no major remodeling or repairs. There was no clear storage system, the walls were corroded and moldy, and we were running out of space.

As a result I spearheaded a renovation of the Purdue Sigbots lab by removing outdated equipment, repairing and painting walls, and installing upgraded storage including shelves, cupboards, and large drawers. To raise funds I acquired sponsorships and donations. The plan was put together in CAD with Fusion 360 and budgeted in Excel.

Event Planning

To bring in a source of income, we host two to three high school robotics competitions each year. We also host the largest VEXU competition, aside from worlds, in the world. These events not only support the team financially but also reinforce the Purdue SigBots role as a leader in the global robotics community. Part of my job as the mechanics lead I am responsible for designing and fabricating the trophies awarded at these competitions.

Portable Field Raisers

One of the struggles of hosting a tournament is setting up and taking down fields. For world qualifying events it is required that they must be raised two feet off the ground. Me and a peer were commissioned to create a collapsible field that could cut setup time in half while also being scalable and affordable.
